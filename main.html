Marques de Pompal

“Decades ago, the square actually resembled a market, while with the begining of vehicular transportation it has been reimagined as a kind of massive intersection.The presence of two subway lines (Linha Azul and Linha Amarela) intersecting at stations beneath the surface and at least a dozen city and private bus routes attest to the importance of this transportation hub. Within the reach of the square there are many banks and luxury hotels.

On the north side there are well-maintained lawns, hedges, shrubs in Eduardo VII Park, which provides a beautiful view of the Baixa district and the longest Iberian river, Tagus. Note to remember: In Eduardo VII Park there is a beautiful greenhouse that is called Estufa Fria. If you have time it is definitely worth seeing! The landscape of the square is undoubtedly dominated by a huge monument of just under thirty-six meters in height dedicated to the Marquis de Pombal. The monument, built between 1917 and 1934, was the result of an intense collaboration between two native architects, Adães Bermudes and António Couto.
